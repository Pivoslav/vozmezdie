### Action-Focused Language

**Purpose / function:** Emphasizes actions, operations, and execution, often to convey decisiveness, control, or effectiveness.

**Typical use / examples:** measures were carried out; continues to carry out measures; launched attacks; monitoring is underway

### Ideological Framing (Discrediting)

**Purpose / function:** Ideologically charged language used explicitly to delegitimize, discredit, or attack specific people, groups, activities, or events.

**Typical use / examples:** fascist accomplices; anti-Soviet provocations; reactionary course; religious fanatics

### Ideological Phrasing (Normalizing)

**Purpose / function:** Ideological language that channels interpretation and values without directly attacking or discrediting a specific target.

**Typical use / examples:** publications on the “famine”; testimony from “eyewitnesses”; increasing political vigilance; patriotic and internationalist upbringing; toilers

### Institutional / Bureaucratic Lingo

**Purpose / function:** Formulaic language specific to KGB and Soviet administrative reporting practices. Often procedural and opaque.

**Typical use / examples:** operational opportunities; KGB informed; measures were pushed out; line ministries were notified
